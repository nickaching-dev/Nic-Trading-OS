# Risk And Behavior Fundamentals

This note keeps the most useful beginner lessons from the older materials, adapted for the NicOS trading module.

## Risk Tolerance vs Risk Capacity

- Risk tolerance is how much stress or volatility you can emotionally handle.
- Risk capacity is how much risk your real situation can afford.

These are not the same.

Example:

- You may feel brave enough to take a volatile trade.
- But if the size is too large for your account, your risk capacity is still low.

## Volatility

- Volatility means prices can move sharply.
- Volatility is normal.
- Volatility does not automatically mean the idea is good or bad.

For the trading module:

- Do not confuse a fast move with a high-quality setup.
- Do not assume a red candle means the market is broken.

## Diversification In A Trading Context

Diversification does not remove risk, but it can reduce the damage from one bad idea.

For a beginner trader, this usually means:

- do not put too much into one name
- do not let one setup control the month
- do not treat one stock as the whole plan

## Behavior Traps

Common traps:

- FOMO
- overconfidence
- panic selling
- boredom trading
- revenge trading
- complexity for its own sake

## Fraud And Hype Red Flags

Be careful around:

- guaranteed returns
- act now pressure
- secret strategy claims
- copy-my-trades framing
- screenshots without process
- heavy promotion of leverage without risk discussion

## The Key Beginner Truth

The goal is not to look aggressive. The goal is to stay clear-headed, protect capital, and follow a repeatable process.

## Reflection Questions

- Was I trading a setup, or just reacting to movement?
- Did I size the trade for my account, or for my ego?
- Did I respect the difference between confirmation and hope?
