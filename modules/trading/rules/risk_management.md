# Risk Management

## Purpose

Risk management exists to keep one trade from damaging the whole learning process.

## Account Rules

- The `Moomoo` account is for learning swing trading.
- Long-term ETF money stays separate.
- Start small while learning.
- Keep some cash available for better setups.

## Per-Trade Risk Rule

- Risk only `0.5%` to `1%` of the account on one trade.
- If the setup is very volatile or unclear, reduce size or skip it.

## Simple Position Sizing Formula

Use this basic formula:

`position size = dollar risk / (entry price - stop price)`

Example:

- Account risk allowed: `USD 100`
- Entry: `USD 50`
- Stop: `USD 48`
- Risk per share: `USD 2`
- Position size: `50 shares`

## Reward Rule

- Do not take a trade unless the planned reward is at least `2x` the planned risk.
- If the target is too close and the stop is too wide, skip the trade.

## Open Position Rules

- Keep the number of open positions small while learning.
- Focus on quality, not quantity.
- Do not add to a losing trade just because the stock looks cheaper.

## Loss Control Rules

- If the stop is hit, exit.
- Do not move the stop farther away just to avoid taking a loss.
- If support breaks and the setup is no longer valid, the trade idea is wrong.

## Behavior Rules

- No FOMO entries.
- No revenge trading.
- No trades without a written plan.
- No turning a swing trade into a long-term hold just because it went red.

## Review Rules

After each trade, review:

- Did I follow the plan?
- Was the setup actually clear?
- Was the risk worth the reward?
- Did emotion affect the decision?

## Reminder

A small controlled loss is part of trading. A large uncontrolled loss is usually a broken rule.
