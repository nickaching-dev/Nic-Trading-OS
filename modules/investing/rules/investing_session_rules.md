# Investing Session Rules

## Workspace Boundary

- Use `📈 Bobby | Investments` for investing learning, stock research, chart analysis, long-term stock theses, swing-trade planning notes, and risk-management context.
- Store distilled knowledge here, not raw transcripts.

## Core Separation

- Keep speculative swing-trade candidates separate from long-term individual-stock investments.
- A hypothetical paper trade is not a real trade.

## Canonical Source Rule

- Treat `WOLF` as a speculative swing-trade candidate.
- Keep the canonical live `WOLF` watchlist and current position state in `modules/trading/watchlist/WOLF.md`.
- Keep the July 2 `43` to `52` setup classified as a hypothetical paper-trade review only.

## ASX Rule

- Treat `ASX` as a long-term individual-stock investment.
- Evaluate `ASX` through business performance, earnings, and thesis metrics rather than swing-trade logic.

## Chart Rules

- Treat support and resistance as zones, not exact prices.
- Slight movement above a level is not enough confirmation.
- Use daily charts for swing structure and shorter timeframes for entry refinement.
- Wait for price reaction, candle confirmation, and volume where possible.
- Daily volume bars show participation and context, not automatic proof of buy-versus-sell pressure by themselves.
- Read gaps, candle colour, bodies, and wicks together instead of treating any one feature as decisive.

## Risk Rules

- Never convert a failed swing trade into a long-term hold without a new thesis.
- Closing a losing long and opening a new short are separate decisions.
- A failed long thesis does not automatically create a short thesis.
- Do not short merely because a chart already looks bearish.
- Avoid chasing shorts directly into visible support after an extended decline.
- Prefer a confirmed support breakdown, strong selling volume, and a failed retest before shorting.
- Require a predefined invalidation point and preferably at least `2:1` reward-to-risk.
- Remaining in cash is a valid decision after closing.

## Execution-Cost Rule

- Check commissions, currency-conversion costs, and possible margin exposure before placing small or foreign-market orders.
