## Mission

- Build a life I can enter every day.
- Material success, wealth, status, and relationships should support this mission rather than replace it.

## Weekly Modes

### Architect Mode

- Use this during the Sunday review.
- Review evidence.
- Note what worked.
- Note what felt forced.
- Make one small adjustment.

### Operator Mode

- Use this during the rest of the week.
- Live the current system.
- Collect evidence.
- Avoid redesigning from boredom or temporary emotion.

## Operating Rules

- Do not optimize what has not been lived.
- Nothing becomes a permanent NicOS rule until it has been tested in real life.
- Ask: "Is the system broken, or is Nic simply bored?"
- Repetition and boredom are expected parts of mastery.

## Habit Principles

- Build from identity.
- Track actions separately from mood.
- Use minimum viable versions on difficult days.
- Missing once is human. Return quickly and avoid missing twice.
- Small promises build self-trust.
- Workdays and off-days both need anchors.
- A day off means lower demands, not zero standards.

## Daily Anchor Rule

- Keep full and minimum versions of morning and night anchors available.
- Use trigger design so the next good action is easier to start when energy is low.

## Sleep Learning

- Shutdown, lower-stimulation evenings, breathing, and prayer appear to support better sleep.
- Late heavy meals and accidental sleep patterns tend to make sleep worse.
- The most important habit is intentional closure.
- Track sleep quality, energy, and mood in the morning, and keep context notes because they matter more than isolated scores.

## Off-Day Rule

- Lack of structure increases the risk of drifting, rumination, and abandoning routines.
- Do not solve that problem by working constantly.
- Build the ability to create alignment without needing external pressure.
- On off-days, keep:
  - a morning anchor
  - one meaningful block
  - one enjoyable or life-giving activity
  - an intentional evening closure

## Rumination Boundary

- Empty time can become rumination if it is not lightly structured.
- Reflection should have a limit.
- After a short period of feeling or writing, change state or environment.
