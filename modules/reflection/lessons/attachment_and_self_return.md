## Core Lessons

- Connection can pause without the relationship disappearing.
- A future partner must not replace self-regulation.
- Nic can love deeply without abandoning himself.
- External validation should not become the final proof of worth.

## Fear Framework

- Separate facts, fears, interpretations, and evidence.
- A fear can be emotionally loud without being new information.
- Trust should be the default unless evidence says otherwise.
- Learn to tolerate uncertainty instead of controlling through checking, over-texting, accusations, or reassurance-seeking.

## Bedtime Relationship Standard

- Connect intentionally.
- Say goodnight clearly.
- Keep a short personal wind-down.
- Sleep without requiring one more message.

## Personal Anchors To Keep

- Sleep rituals
- Work and financial goals
- Friendships and family
- Faith
- Health
- Learning
- Time alone

## Self-Return Rule

- "If someone chooses differently, I return to me."
- Another person's decision may hurt, but it does not determine worth.
- Do not use independence language as armor against vulnerability.

## Confidence Learning

- Work built real competence, endurance, leadership, and value confidence.
- Relationship insecurity activates the separate question of being lovable or chosen.
- Work confidence is real, but incomplete.
- The next stage is confidence that still holds during vulnerability and uncertainty.

## Response To Ex-Related Thoughts

- Do not fight the thought or treat it like a command.
- Name the feeling accurately.
- Ask what is actually being missed.
- Treat dreams and intrusive memories as processing, not predictions or hidden messages.
- Measure progress by how the thought is handled, not by whether it appears.
- Return attention to one action that supports the life being built.
