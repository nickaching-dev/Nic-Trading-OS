# Reference Sources

Use these when you want low-hype learning material or a reality check.

## Best Source Types

1. Investor-protection and regulator education
2. Large educational libraries from major firms
3. Structured, low-hype learning resources
4. Evidence-based teachers who explain process clearly

## Good Starting Points

- [Investor.gov](https://www.investor.gov/)
  Good for basics, risk, diversification, and fraud awareness.

- [FINRA Investing Basics](https://www.finra.org/investors/investing/investing-basics)
  Good for investor protection, risk, and plain-English explanations.

- [Charles Schwab: New to Investing](https://www.schwab.com/learn/new-to-investing/)
  Good for structured beginner education.

- [Fidelity Learning Center](https://www.fidelity.com/learning-center/trading-investing/investing-for-beginners)
  Good for practical beginner reading.

- [Khan Academy: Finance and Capital Markets](https://www.khanacademy.org/economics-finance-domain/core-finance)
  Good for slow, step-by-step explanations.

## Source Filter

Prefer sources that:

- explain process
- discuss risk honestly
- show tradeoffs
- avoid urgency and hype

Avoid sources that:

- guarantee results
- sell certainty
- push alerts over understanding
- rely on screenshots instead of reasoning
