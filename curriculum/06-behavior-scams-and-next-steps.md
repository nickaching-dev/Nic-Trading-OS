# Lesson 6: Behavior, Scams, and Staying the Course

## Goal

Learn the habits that protect long-term investors from their own emotions and from bad actors.

## The emotional side of investing

Most people do not fail because they never heard the word "ETF."

They fail because:

- they panic during drops
- they chase what recently went up
- they get bored and overtrade
- they mistake confidence for skill

## Common behavior traps

- FOMO
- recency bias
- overconfidence
- panic selling
- performance chasing
- complexity for its own sake

## Fraud red flags

Be very careful when you hear:

- "guaranteed returns"
- "risk-free"
- "everyone is getting in"
- "act now"
- "secret strategy"
- "I made this much in one week"

## Healthy long-term habits

- automate contributions
- check costs
- keep an emergency fund
- diversify broadly
- review periodically, not constantly
- write a plan before emotions show up

## What "staying the course" means

It does not mean never thinking.

It means:

- changing strategy for good reasons, not for drama
- reviewing your plan on purpose
- not letting headlines control your portfolio

## Your next step after this course

You should be ready to build a simple written investing plan that covers:

- goal
- time horizon
- account type
- target allocation
- contribution habit
- rebalance rule
- panic-prevention rule
