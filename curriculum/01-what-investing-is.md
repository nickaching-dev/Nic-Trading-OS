# Lesson 1: What Investing Is

## Goal

Understand what investing is, what the stock market does, and why time matters.

## The big idea

Investing means using money today to buy assets that you expect to grow in value or pay you cash over time.

## Saving vs. investing

- Saving is for money you may need soon and want to keep stable.
- Investing is for money you do not need soon and want to grow over years.

A savings account protects short-term money. Investing accepts uncertainty in exchange for a chance at higher long-term growth.

## What the stock market is

The stock market is a marketplace where people buy and sell ownership in companies.

When you buy a stock, you are buying a small ownership stake in a business. If that business grows profits and other investors value it more highly, the stock price can rise. Some companies also pay dividends, which are cash payments to shareholders.

## Where returns come from

A stock investor can make money from:

- price appreciation
- dividends

A bond investor can make money from:

- interest payments
- return of principal at maturity

## Why investing exists

Companies raise money to grow. Investors provide capital in exchange for a claim on future cash flows. The market connects those two sides.

## Why time matters

Time horizon is one of the most important ideas in investing.

- If you need the money in 1 to 3 years, stock-market risk can be too high.
- If you need the money in 10, 20, or 30 years, stock investing may be much more reasonable.

Short time horizon and stock investing are often a bad mix.

## Why compounding matters

Compounding means your money can earn returns on past returns, not just on the original amount you put in.

This is why starting early and staying consistent matters so much.

## Beginner mistakes to avoid

- treating investing like fast money
- putting emergency-fund money into stocks
- copying random stock picks online
- assuming a rising market means you are skilled

## Mini recap

- Saving protects short-term money.
- Investing grows long-term money.
- Stocks are slices of businesses.
- The stock market is a marketplace for ownership.
- Time horizon changes what is appropriate to invest in.
