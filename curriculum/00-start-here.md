# Start Here

This course is for someone with zero investing knowledge.

## What you will learn, in order

1. What investing is and why people do it
2. What the stock market is
3. What stocks, bonds, funds, ETFs, and index funds are
4. How risk, diversification, and asset allocation work
5. Why fees, taxes, and behavior matter so much
6. How to make a simple first investing plan

## What this course will not do first

- It will not start with stock tips.
- It will not start with chart reading.
- It will not start with options, leverage, or trading strategies.
- It will not pretend investing is easy money.

## Ground rules

- Ask "what is that?" every time a term is unclear.
- Move one lesson at a time.
- Do not invest money you need soon.
- Do not invest before building basic financial stability.

## Before serious investing

You should generally have:

- a basic emergency fund
- no high-interest debt emergency
- a reason for investing
- a rough time horizon for when the money will be needed

## Your first target outcome

By the end of this course, you should be able to explain:

- what a stock is
- why diversified funds exist
- why long-term investors care about costs and behavior
- why a beginner often starts with broad-market investing instead of stock picking
