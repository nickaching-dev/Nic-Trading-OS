# Swing Trading Rules

## Purpose

- This playbook is for learning a repeatable swing-trading process.
- My long-term ETF investing stays separate from my trading account.
- My first goal is to protect capital and build skill, not to force fast profits.

## Account Rules

- Use only money that is set aside for swing trading.
- Keep some cash available for better setups instead of being fully invested all the time.
- Start small while learning.
- Risk only `0.5%` to `1%` of the trading account on any single trade.
- Never add to a losing trade just because the price looks cheaper.

## What I Trade

- Stocks with a clear trend or a clear range.
- Charts where support and resistance can be marked simply.
- Setups with a reason I can explain in one or two sentences.
- Stocks that I have reviewed before the trade, not random names found in the moment.

## Entry Rules

- Check the trend first: uptrend, downtrend, or range.
- Mark support and resistance before thinking about an entry.
- Prefer entries near support, not in the middle of nowhere.
- Wait for confirmation before entering.
- Confirmation can mean a clean bounce, a hold above support, or a constructive close.
- Do not buy blindly just because price touched support once.
- If the setup is unclear, skip the trade.

## Risk and Reward Rules

- Define the entry, stop, and target before entering.
- Put the stop where the trade idea is clearly wrong.
- Take trades only when the planned reward is at least `2x` the planned risk.
- If support fails and the setup breaks, exit without debating.

## Exit Rules

- Exit if price breaks the level that made the trade valid.
- Take profit into planned resistance or target zones.
- Do not turn a short-term trade into a long-term hold just because it is red.
- If a stock moves sharply into target on a news-driven spike, it is okay to pay yourself.

## Behavior Rules

- No FOMO entries after a large move.
- No revenge trading after a loss.
- No trade is required every day or every week.
- Journal every trade, even the ones that do not work.
- Review whether I followed the plan, not just whether I made money.

## Simple Review Questions

- Was the trend clear?
- Did I buy confirmation or make a guess?
- Was the reward worth the risk?
- Did I respect my stop?
- Would I take the same trade again under the same conditions?

## Reminder

This playbook is for education and process-building. It is not a promise of profit or personalized financial advice.
