# Source Library

Curated on June 8, 2026.

## How to use this list

- Start with the "Core reading" section.
- Use YouTube as reinforcement, not as your only teacher.
- If a video contradicts a regulator or investor-protection source, trust the regulator source first.

## Core reading

### Investor protection and fundamentals

- [Investor.gov: Asset Allocation and Diversification](https://www.investor.gov/introduction-investing/getting-started/asset-allocation)
  Why it matters: clear beginner explanation of time horizon, risk tolerance, and diversification.

- [Investor.gov: What is compound interest?](https://www.investor.gov/additional-resources/information/youth/teachers-classroom-resources/what-compound-interest)
  Why it matters: shows why starting early matters.

- [Investor.gov: Index Funds](https://www.investor.gov/introduction-investing/investing-basics/investment-products/mutual-funds-and-exchange-traded-4)
  Why it matters: explains how index funds work, what they cost, and what risks remain.

- [Investor.gov: What You Can Do to Avoid Investment Fraud](https://www.investor.gov/protect-your-investments/fraud/how-avoid-fraud/what-you-can-do-avoid-investment-fraud)
  Why it matters: beginners are frequent scam targets.

- [FINRA: Investing Basics](https://www.finra.org/investors/investing/investing-basics)
  Why it matters: strong overview of goals, buying and selling, risk, fees, and diversification.

- [FINRA: Risk](https://www.finra.org/investors/investing/investing-basics/risk)
  Why it matters: helps connect higher expected return with higher uncertainty.

- [FINRA: Asset Allocation and Diversification](https://www.finra.org/investors/investing/investing-basics/asset-allocation-diversification)
  Why it matters: practical guidance on spreading risk and rebalancing.

### Major institution education

- [Vanguard: What is an index fund?](https://investor.vanguard.com/investor-resources-education/understanding-investment-types/what-is-an-index-fund)
  Why it matters: good beginner explanation of index funds and why they are often low-cost.

- [Fidelity: Investing for beginners](https://www.fidelity.com/learning-center/trading-investing/investing-for-beginners)
  Why it matters: broad beginner hub with plain-English articles on brokerage accounts, ETFs, risk tolerance, diversification, and first investments.

- [Fidelity: Risk tolerance - 3 key questions](https://www.fidelity.com/learning-center/personal-finance/risk-tolerance-time-horizon)
  Why it matters: separates emotional tolerance, finances, and timeline.

- [Charles Schwab: Investing basics FAQs](https://www.schwab.com/how-to-invest/investing-basics)
  Why it matters: strong beginner material on compounding, risk, and diversification.

- [Charles Schwab: New to Investing](https://www.schwab.com/learn/new-to-investing/)
  Why it matters: one of the best low-hype beginner video hubs.

- [Khan Academy: Finance and Capital Markets](https://www.khanacademy.org/economics-finance-domain/core-finance)
  Why it matters: nonprofit, curriculum-style basics if you want slower, step-by-step explanations.

### Philosophy and portfolio basics

- [Bogleheads University 101 PDF](https://boglecenter.net/wp-content/uploads/2024-Bogleheads-University-101.pdf)
  Why it matters: structured beginner-friendly material on investing language, simple portfolios, and common mistakes.

- [Bogleheads About Us](https://www.bogleheads.org/AboutUs.htm)
  Why it matters: good jumping-off point into the Bogleheads education ecosystem.

### U.S. account and safety basics

- [IRS: Individual Retirement Arrangements (IRAs)](https://www.irs.gov/retirement-plans/individual-retirement-arrangements-iras)
  Why it matters: official IRA rules, rollovers, and withdrawal basics.

- [U.S. Department of Labor: Savings Fitness](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/ebsa/about-ebsa/our-activities/resource-center/publications/savings-fitness)
  Why it matters: federal guide to retirement saving habits and workplace plans.

- [NASAA: Investor Education](https://www.nasaa.org/investor-education/)
  Why it matters: strong fraud-awareness material from state securities regulators.

- [SIPC: What SIPC Protects](https://www.sipc.org/for-investors/what-sipc-protects)
  Why it matters: explains what brokerage protection does and does not cover.

- [FDIC: Financial Products That Are Not Insured](https://www.fdic.gov/resources/deposit-insurance/financial-products-not-insured)
  Why it matters: prevents the common mistake of assuming investments sold by banks are market-loss protected.

## How to use YouTube safely

- Prefer channels that explain principles, not picks.
- Be skeptical of people selling courses, alerts, or private groups.
- Ignore flashy profit screenshots.
- Avoid making decisions from a single video.
- Cross-check big claims with Investor.gov, FINRA, or a major institution education page.

## Strong beginner rule

If a creator spends more time selling certainty than teaching risk, leave.
